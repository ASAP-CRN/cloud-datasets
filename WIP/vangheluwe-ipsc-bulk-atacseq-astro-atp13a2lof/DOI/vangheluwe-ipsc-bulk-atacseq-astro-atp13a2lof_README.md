# "Chromatin accessibility profiling (ATAC-seq) of human iPSC-derived midbrain astrocytes in ATP13A2 c.1306 deficiency models".

## Dataset Description:

This dataset contains bulk Assay for Transposase-Accessible Chromatin using sequencing (ATAC-seq) data from human iPSC-derived midbrain astrocytes. The study compares Wild Type (WT) astrocytes and ATP13A2 c.1306 loss-of-function mutants. The objective was to characterize the epigenomic landscape and changes in genome-wide accessibility driven by the genotype.

**ASAP Project:** Implications of Polyamine and Glucosylceramide Transport for Parkinson's Disease (IMPACT-PD)

**Project Description:** Our research program investigates the molecular mechanisms of Parkinson's disease (PD) by dissecting how PD-associated genes—specifically ATP13A2, ATP10B, and GBA1—regulate lysosomal homeostasis. As part of the ASAP Collaborative Research Network (CRN), our team employs a multi-disciplinary approach—integrating human iPSC-derived midbrain models, rodent systems, and purified protein complexes—to determine how lysosomal dysfunction triggers cellular states associated with neurodegeneration. A primary focus of this project is characterizing the transition of astrocytes toward a reactive, pro-inflammatory state and the subsequent impact on dopaminergic neuron survival. By generating large-scale transcriptomic (RNA-seq), chromatin accessibility (ATAC-seq), and methylome (bisulfite sequencing) datasets, we aim to map the pathways linking genetic loss-of-function (e.g., ATP13A2 c.1306) to chronic neuroinflammation and impaired lysosomal proteolysis. Ultimately, this work seeks to identify central regulators of these pathogenic states to provide new therapeutic strategies for PD.
